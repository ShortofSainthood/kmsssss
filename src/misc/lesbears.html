As a fat, hairy, masculine-leaning lesbian, I've always felt a connection to the bear label and wished that lesbians had our own equivalent to bear brotherhood. A lot of WLW representation revolves around thin, hairless, traditionally feminine women. I'm not saying it's bad to be any of those things, but not all of us are like that, or want to be like that. It makes people like me feel left out. However, I still felt like it'd be disingenuous to just straight up call myself a bear, since I'm not a man or attracted to men.

Around 2020ish, I was on Tumblr one day and saw someone sent an ask to a blog I followed. The ask mentioned hearing about "Ursula Lesbians" at a pride event they attended earlier that day, "Ursulas" being a female/WLW counterpart to bears. I was both very excited and very skeptical when I read this. As someone who used the site for over a decade I can attest that Tumblr users lie about shit all the fucking time. Especially about historical information.

I was pleasantly surprised when I did a little googling and saw that their claims actually held some weight. With the gay and lesbian communities intermingling so much, there are historic accounts of lesbians calling themselves bears (or bear equivalents, like Ursulas) and engaging in bear culture. Unfortunately, these sources are few and far between.

This page is my attempt at cataloging and archiving as many sources on women, especially women who love other women, in the bear community as possible. I'd love to eventually compile this into a coherent essay or timeline, but this is all I have for now.
